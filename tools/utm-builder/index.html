What is a UTM Builder?

A UTM Builder adds tracking parameters to a URL so analytics platforms can attribute clicks to the right channel and campaign. It‚Äôs a simple way to measure performance across social profiles, video descriptions, newsletters, ads, QR codes, and partner mentions.

Which UTM fields should you use?

Most tracking setups work best with the ‚Äúcore three‚Äù: utm_source, utm_medium, utm_campaign. Use utm_content to separate placements (bio vs description) or variants (A/B tests). Use utm_term mainly for paid search keywords or ad groups.

How to name UTMs (clean + consistent)

Keep values short, lowercase, and consistent across channels. Avoid spaces (use hyphens). Example: utm_source=instagram, utm_medium=social, utm_campaign=tools-directory, utm_content=bio.

Avoid inconsistent naming (instagram vs ig vs insta). Don‚Äôt put full sentences in UTMs. Keep the campaign stable across platforms, then separate placements with utm_content. If your destination URL already has parameters, this tool preserves them and appends UTMs correctly.
